The more you know: Identity management in the Power Platform

December 22, 2025 - Vulnerability Write-up

Sometimes you misunderstand how a product is designed, which can lead to insecure defaults. This definitely happened to me recently with the Power Pages product.

This is the story of how a simple anti-pattern in Power Platform identity management might lead you to expose authenticated pages to unexpected people.

The setup

The architecture is pretty straightforward. When you create Power Pages, they are part of an "environment". It is standard practice to have an environment shared between many sites.

You might have a dev environment and a production environment for your corp, for example.

Some pages can be protected by the "Authenticated Users" role. This is the default setting to protect pages.

You might want to put internal pages here, perhaps an internal wiki or something restricted to partners. As a security measure, you disable "open registration" which is a site level setting. You may naturally assume that because open registration is closed, nobody from the internet can access those pages.

"Authenticated users" default role

"Open registration" page level settings that control if an anonymous usercan create a new local user account

So, what's up?

Here is the catch: registration work at the environment level.

This assumption holds true only if you don't have other sites that are meant to be public in the same environment.

I was really surprised to learn that even if you close open registration on Site A, a user can still be registered cross-site if they register on Site B within the same environment. Because the identity lives at the environment level, they are technically an "Authenticated User" for both.

This doesn't seem to be a vulnerability on Microsoft's side; it is a product design choice. However, it is a nuance that is easy to miss and that might create dangerous gap between expectation and reality. I wanted to share this so nobody else makes the same assumptions I did.

The conclusion

As usual, always make sure your threat model is in line with your provider's actual architecture.

Do not forget to test security assumptions internally or delegate that to a trusted partner. And, I guess, don't trust the GUI implicitly.

Oh, and maybe the solution would be to eventually put the "Disable Open Registration" option as an environment setting and not a site setting as the mechanic actually affects the environment and not the site.

Thanks for reading everyone!
